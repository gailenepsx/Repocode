The simulator shall be adapted to support monthly alert distribution for the impacted models FTF, TSD, and EFT. This change shall apply at distribution level only and shall not modify the existing detection logic or the hit-generation rules.
For these models, daily hits detected within the same calendar month shall be grouped into a single monthly alert based on the relevant alert_date. The existing ALERT_ID field in the output files shall be reused to represent this monthly alert group, so that multiple transactions belonging to the same month may share the same ALERT_ID.
To support business traceability, a new field named month shall be added to the output alert files in order to identify the month associated with each alert. In addition, the current daily alert identifier shall be retained for tracking and debugging purposes in a new field named daily_alert_id.
The functional impact shall apply to all relevant output files generated by the simulator. This includes the Alert files, Alert Notes files, Alert Steps files, and Audit Trail files, all of which shall reflect the monthly grouping logic and the introduction of the new tracking fields.
For FTF, TSD, and EFT, the expected result is that alerts will no longer be distributed as separate daily alert occurrences when they belong to the same month. Instead, they shall be distributed as one monthly alert containing the underlying daily hits grouped under a common monthly ALERT_ID, while preserving the original daily-level reference through daily_alert_id.
